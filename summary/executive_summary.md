# Executive Summary

## Objective
This analysis evaluated the user journey from signup to activation and Day 30 retention for a SaaS platform. The goal was to identify where onboarding friction occurs, determine which user segments provide the strongest long-term value, and recommend actions to improve user retention.

## Key Questions
1. Where do users drop off during onboarding?
2. Which channels and segments drive the strongest retention?
3. Which early behaviors are associated with long-term engagement?
4. What actions should the business prioritize next?

## Summary of Findings
The analysis indicates that the primary issue is not user acquisition volume, but weak conversion from signup to meaningful product usage. The largest funnel drop-off occurs before users complete their first core product action, suggesting that onboarding friction is limiting activation.

Retention analysis also shows that acquisition quality varies meaningfully by channel. Some channels generate strong signup volume but weaker Day 30 retention, while referral and organic traffic tend to produce more durable engagement. This suggests that top-of-funnel efficiency should be evaluated alongside downstream retention outcomes.

Early product behavior appears to be a strong signal of long-term value. Users who complete important setup steps and create their first report soon after signup retain at a much higher rate than users who delay adoption of core product workflows.

## Recommendations
1. Simplify onboarding and reduce steps required to reach the first high-value action.
2. Trigger lifecycle interventions for users who have not activated within the first 3 days.
3. Increase investment in channels that deliver stronger Day 30 retention, not just lower acquisition cost.
4. Promote high-value features earlier in the user journey.
5. Test onboarding variants for segments with strong activation potential but weak long-term retention.

## Business Implication
Improving activation quality is likely to increase retained users more effectively than focusing only on acquisition growth. By reducing onboarding friction and prioritizing higher-retention segments, the business can improve user engagement, downstream conversion, and overall efficiency of growth investment.

## Suggested Next Steps
- Launch an onboarding experiment focused on the first core action
- Build a recurring retention dashboard for leadership review
- Monitor retention by acquisition channel and segment monthly
- Extend the analysis to paid conversion and revenue outcomes
